ZVerse Artist Network

Who is ZVerse?

ZVerse is an applications company focused on accelerating the creation of meaningful 3D printable content. With the LAYR 3D Content Creation Platform, ZVerse is creating a suite of software applications that make any 2D content 3D printable. Since being founded in 2012, ZVerse’s technology has brought meaningful, high-quality 3D printed content to brands and organizations all over the world. ZVerse is making the world’s content 3D printable.

What is the objective of the application?

As part of their “turnkey” 3D printing service, ZVerse manages a growing 3D artist network. Until now, ZVerse has managed artist interactions on a case-by-case basis. However, one-on-one management isn’t scalable with new business opportunities. The goal of the app is to create a scalable artist network.

Who is the target audience and what content are they looking for?

The target audience includes 3D artists who wish to work with ZVerse. The information and content should enable the artist to view potential projects, keep track of current projects, maintain a library of completed projects, and choose how they are compensated for completed projects.

Wireframes

In what order should information be presented?

Who is using the network and what do they want to do with it? As a freelance artist, I believe that the primary concern would be to alert the project managers as to whether or not I am available to take on projects.

On Login, available projects are viewed first, with current projects directly beneath this information.

Projects and Billing bookend the navigation links as the most important content, with secondary content in between.

Integrations

Google Fonts:

Lato. A very simple font, in keeping with the current ZVerse website design. Lato is an approachable font chosen for this app because of it’s warm feeling, humanist character despite its sans serif designation.

Responsive:

An approach to design and development that adapts to the users environment based on screen size, platform, and or orientation. Using flexible layouts and employing appropriate CSS media queries, the design of a responsive site adapts to its environment and conveys its content regardless of screen size. Responsive development eliminates the need to design for individual devices.

Markup

Using HTML, SCSS, and JQuery the site is then built beginning with mobile sizes and using media queries to adjust how the content is displayed at different media sizes.
